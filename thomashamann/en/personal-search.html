About my search

A week ago I returned from a trip in the Ticino Alps. A lovely Swiss friend, who is an experienced therapist named Catherine, my wife Johanna and I accompanied a group of people doing a one-week “visionquest” in the mountains. To receive our hidden, inner pictures we used, amongst other methods, the language of our dreams and their picturesque landscapes.

During these mild late summer days in the midst of the untouched powerful mountains and beyond all worldly hurly-burly, more and more phases of my life came to me. My seeking and longing for value and meaning in my life, my detours and wrong tracks in a world which was falling apart, had not much in store for me anymore.

“What is the sense of my life?
What is beyond the facades of the world? Who am I?” were the basic questions for mankind and myself.

I knew as if I always somewhat sensed that I am here on this earth to experience joy and freedom as a birthright – even if I did not feel good or if I struggled on the surface of my actual life situation. I needed to become aware that alcohol, excesses and other drugs were an expression of my longing and thirst.

I got busy with magic and symbolism, contacted the Freemasonry and the Rosicrucians and followed the path of the Shamans. Burning my feet walking on fire, taking part in “visionquests” and sweating in the sweat lodges, finally led me to differentiate between reality and illusion. In the past seven years, I was close to my spiritual teacher who brought me in contact with somewhat uncomfortable and suffering concepts of myself and life, parallel to my deeper insights and expansion.

Along the way, art has always been a diary of my emotions, a photo of each situation in my life. Art has been my path ever since, and has constantly led me closer to myself and has been a steady medicine for my soul.

In my work, I have felt and frequently feel extremely attracted by the footpaths of mankind until now. This, in addition to the mystery of life and death. In my studio I surround myself with enlargements of cave paintings to get sensitised for their emotional meaning.

Working on a new painting, it is impossible for me not to break the surface in order to discover the unknown that would aim to the secret behind all things. Whether my new work is satisfying or not, depends on how my soul resonates and how my yearning is touched.

My discovery serves the need to extend my limitations in art and in life, continuously yearning for more freedom, light heartedness and authenticity in expression. Often it seems that the path is full of stones and hard to work on, at other times everything flows freely and easily.

After all that, I can say with knowing that what I am in my being is stillness! The expression is joy, light heartedness, power and incorporates all creation of life.

… deep down I found out what I already knew in the innermost me… therefore art is a vehicle to express passion and the joy of life and deliver it to the world.

Thomas Hamann - September 2007
